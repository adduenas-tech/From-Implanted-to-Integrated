The Problem

When a district contracts with an ABA agency, the typical model places a BCBA supervising an RBT in a 1:1 relationship with one student. The BCBA's focus stays within that dyad â€” while the classroom, teaching team, and school ecosystem remain largely untouched. Research calls this an "additive" or siloed model. The result: interventions that work in the bubble but don't generalize, and school staff who feel excluded rather than empowered.
